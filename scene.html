The crime scene and the evidence

Detectives investigated the crime scene for about one hour and were unable to link Carlos De Luna to any forensic evidence. Store transaction logs suggest no money was taken from the Sigmor station store.

Eyewitness

Kevan Baker saw a struggle inside the station and then saw a Hispanic man exit and run around to the back of the station. Later that night he identified the man as De Luna. Baker now says he is not sure the man he saw was De Luna.

Fingerprints

Detectives did not find fingerprints on the weapon, the phone, the counter or a pack of cigarettes, which the killer may have handled.

Knife

The weapon, a lock-blade knife, was Hernandezs trademark, according to a former Corpus Christi detective. Police reports identify similar weapons in several crimes Hernandez committed.

Blood

A large amount of blood was spattered on the floor and counter, but police did not collect any for samples and did not find any on De Lunas clothing.

Safe

Prosecutors said De Luna robbed the station when he killed Lopez, citing $149 in DeLunas pocket at the time of his capture. But a review of the stations financial records shows that scenario was unlikely. A station log shows that Lopez deposited $100 into the safe just 38 minutes before she was killed. Because $55 in cash was found in the station, she would have had to have taken in $204 in that 38-minute period without dropping any in the safe.
